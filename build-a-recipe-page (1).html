Schnitzel

Schnitzel is a classic German dish made of breaded and fried meat, usually pork or veal. It is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, perfect for a hearty meal.

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless pork chops (or veal cutlets)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Place each pork chop between two sheets of plastic wrap and gently pound until about 1/4 inch thick.
  2. Season the meat with salt and pepper on both sides.
  3. Set up a breading station: flour in one bowl, beaten eggs in another, and breadcrumbs in a third.
  4. Dredge each chop in flour, dip in egg, and coat with breadcrumbs.
  5. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat and fry the schnitzels for 3-4 minutes on each side until golden brown.
  6. Drain on paper towels and serve hot with lemon wedges.
